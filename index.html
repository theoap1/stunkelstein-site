What is Stunkelstein?

Stunkelstein is a social-deduction party game for in-person groups, played on your phones. Everyone shares the same secret word except one player — the Stunk — who doesn't know it and must blend in. Players give clues, talk, and vote to catch the Stunk. The Stunk wins by surviving the vote or by guessing the secret word.

If you've ever played Spyfall, Undercover, or "the game where one person doesn't know the word," you already know the feeling. Stunkelstein is the named, polished version of that imposter word game — built for a real room: zero setup, short rounds, and a reveal that lands.

How does a round work?

1 · Everyone joins

One person starts a game and shares a 6-character code or QR. Everyone joins on their own phone — no account needed.

2 · Secret roles

Each phone privately reveals a role. Everyone sees the secret word — except the Stunk, who sees nothing and has to fake it.

3 · Talk & bluff

Go around giving clues and asking questions. The Stunk improvises to blend in. The room hunts for who doesn't quite fit.

4 · Vote & reveal

A 90-second vote decides who the room thinks is the Stunk. Then the truth drops — caught, or the Stunk escapes.

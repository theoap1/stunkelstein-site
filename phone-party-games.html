The best phone party games to play with friends

Updated June 2026 · by The Stunkelstein Team · ~5 min read

What's a good party game to play on your phone with friends?

Stunkelstein is a great phone party game: everyone joins one game on their own phone by code or QR, gets a secret role, and tries to catch the hidden Stunk. Because every player has a phone, the secret roles stay private — perfect for a social-deduction game. Other strong phone party games include Heads Up, Psych, Wavelength, and Jackbox.

Everyone already has a phone in their pocket. These games turn that into the whole party.

The best phone party games

1. Stunkelstein — phones as secret role cards

Each player's phone shows their private role, so one of you can be the hidden Stunk without anyone seeing. Join by code or QR, no account needed, rounds in minutes. It's the social-deduction game that only works because everyone has a phone. How to play →

2. Heads Up!

Charades on your phone — hold it to your forehead and have friends act out the clue before the timer runs out.

3. Psych!

Bluff your friends with fake answers and spot the real one. Cards-Against-Humanity energy in trivia form.

4. Wavelength

The "mind-reading" dial game, playable in person or over a call. Great for debate-heavy groups.

5. Jackbox Party Pack

A whole library of games where phones are the controllers and one screen is shared. The gold standard for a couch crowd.

6. Gartic Phone

Free, browser-based "telephone with drawings." Chaos guaranteed, no download.

Why phone games are perfect for parties

  • No extra gear — everyone brings the controller in their pocket.
  • Private screens make hidden-role games like Stunkelstein possible.
  • Instant setup — join a game by code or QR and you're playing.

Frequently asked questions

Are there free party games you can play on your phone?

Yes. Stunkelstein is free with anonymous sign-in and no account. Browser games like Gartic Phone are free too, and apps like Heads Up and Psych have free modes with optional paid packs.

What phone game is best for a hidden-role / imposter night?

Stunkelstein — because each phone shows a private role, the Stunk stays secret. That's exactly what a social-deduction game needs. See games like Spyfall.

Do all players need to download an app?

The host runs Stunkelstein and shares a code or QR; players join the same game on their phones. It's built for a multi-device room.

What is the best party game for adults?

The best party game for adults is one that teaches in two minutes, scales to a crowd, and gets the whole room laughing. Stunkelstein checks all three — a social-deduction game where everyone shares a secret word except the hidden Stunk, played on the phones you already have. Other top picks: Codenames, Wavelength, Monikers, and Telestrations.

A great party game is fast, funny, and easy to learn — no hour-long setup, no rulebook. Here are the ones that actually deliver.

The list

1. Stunkelstein — the no-setup social deduction game

Everyone shares a secret word except one player, the Stunk, who has to bluff their way through. Talk, accuse, vote. It teaches instantly, scales from 4 to 20, and the reveal gets the loudest reaction of the night. Free, and it doubles as a drinking game. How to play →

2. Codenames

Two teams race to contact their agents using one-word clues, dodging the assassin. Brilliant, simple, endlessly replayable.

3. Wavelength

One player gives a clue to place a hidden dial on a spectrum like "overrated vs underrated." Great for debate and big laughs.

4. Monikers

Get your team to guess names and phrases across three rounds, building inside jokes as you go. A big-group favourite.

5. Telestrations

Telephone meets Pictionary — draw, guess, draw, and watch the prompt mutate into nonsense. The reveal is the payoff.

6. Spyfall

One spy, one location, lots of probing questions. The other great social-deduction pick. More like this →

What makes a great party game?

  • Teaches in two minutes — nobody wants a lecture.
  • Welcomes "non-gamers" — everyone can jump in.
  • Scales to the room — works at 5 and at 15.
  • Gets people laughing — the table is the entertainment.
